VOICE
``````
Shows who does the action and who receives the action

🟢 ACTIVE VOICE
subject does the action

subject + verb + object
ex: 
She writes a letter.
The company conducted the test.
vyshu completed the project.

🔵 PASSIVE VOICE

object receives the action 

object + be(is/was/has been) + V3 + by + subject
ex:
A letter is written by her.
The test was conducted by the company.
The project was completed by Vaishnavi.

| Tense              | Active      | Passive          |
| ------------------ | ----------- | ---------------- |
| Present Simple     | writes      | is written       |
| Past Simple        | wrote       | was written      |
| Future             | will write  | will be written  |
| Present Perfect    | has written | has been written |
| Present Continuous | is writing  | is being written |

# 🔑 TENSE STRUCTURES (ONLY WHAT YOU NEED)

Think of tenses like **formulas**, not grammar.

---

## 1️⃣ Simple Present — STRUCTURE

### 👉 Affirmative

```
Subject + V1 (+ s/es for he/she/it)
```

Examples:

* I **go** to college.
* She **goes** to college.

### 👉 Negative

```
Subject + do/does + not + V1
```

* I **do not go**
* She **does not go**

### 👉 Question

```
Do/Does + subject + V1 ?
```

* Do you go?
* Does she go?

---

## 2️⃣ Simple Past — STRUCTURE

### 👉 Affirmative

```
Subject + V2
```

* I **went** home.

### 👉 Negative

```
Subject + did not + V1
```

* I **did not go** home.

### 👉 Question

```
Did + subject + V1 ?
```

* Did you go home?

⚠️ Rule you must remember:
❌ did + V2 (wrong)
✅ did + V1 (correct)

---

## 3️⃣ Simple Future — STRUCTURE

### 👉 Affirmative

```
Subject + will + V1
```

* I **will go** home.

### 👉 Negative

```
Subject + will not + V1
```

* I **will not go** home.

### 👉 Question

```
Will + subject + V1 ?
```

* Will you go home?

---

## 4️⃣ Past Perfect (HAD) — STRUCTURE

### 👉 Affirmative

```
Subject + had + V3
```

* I **had gone** home.

### 👉 Negative

```
Subject + had not + V3
```

* I **had not gone** home.

### 👉 Question

```
Had + subject + V3 ?
```

* Had you gone home?

---

## 🧠 ONE-LINE MEMORY TRICK (VERY IMPORTANT)

* **Present** → V1
* **Past** → V2
* **Future** → will + V1
* **had** → V3

Error correction
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
1. Subject-Verb Agreement --->
Rule: A singular subject takes a singular verb, and a plural subject takes a plural verb.
EX: She (plays) the piano.✅
    She play the piano. ❌

2. Incorrect Use of Tenses --->
The verb tense must match the time of action.
I saw him yesterday ✅
I see him yesterday ❌

3. Wrong Use of Articles (a, an, the) --->
a--> before consonant (e.g., a university)
an--> before vowel (e.g., an apple)
the--> specific things or unique things (e.g., the sun)
ex: She is a doctor. ✅
    She is an doctor. ❌

4. pronoun-Antecedent Agreement --->
Rule: A pronoun must agree with its antecedent in number and gender.
Every student must bring his or her ID card.✅
Every student must bring their ID card. ❌

***5. Misplaced and Dangling Modifiers
Rule: Modifies should be placed next to the word they describe
The man driving the car was wearing a red cap. ✅ 
The man was wearing a red cap driving the car. ❌

6. Parellism (Consistency in sentence Structure)
Rule: Item in a list or series should be in the same grammatical form.
He likes swimming, running, cycling.✅
He likes swimming, running, cycle.❌

7. Double Negatives
Rule: Avoid using two negatives in a sentenc8e
She has nothing to say. ✅
She doesn't have nothing to say.❌

8. Incorrect prepositions
Rule: use appropriate prepositions as per context.
I am interested in music.✅
I am interested on music.❌

9. Redundant Words(Unnecessary Repetition)
Rule: Avoid using words that repeat the same idea.
He returned home ✅ (Remove back)
He returned back home❌

10. Confusing 'Who' vs 'Whom'
who is used as a subject (like 'he')
Whom is used as an object (like 'him')

Who is calling me? ✅ 
whom is calling me? ❌

11. Incorrect Use of "Much" vs "Many"
Rule: 
much for uncountable nouns.
many for countable nouns.

There aren't many books in the library. ✅ 
There aren't much books in the library. ❌

She doesn't have much patience. ✅ 
She doesn't have many patience. ❌

12. Incorrect Use of "Less" vs "Fewer"
Rule:
less for uncountable nouns.
fewer for countable nouns.

There are fewer students in the classroom today. ✅ 
There are less students in the classroom today. ❌

I drank less water today. ✅ 
I drank fewer water today. ❌
